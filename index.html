About

Lo-Ellen Robotics is a team of high school students and mentors from Sudbury, Ontario. Since 2012, our team has designed, developed and built new robots to compete against other world-class teams. Lo-Ellen Robotics has previously competed at the FIRST World Championships and won awards in business, robot design, team leadership, professionalism and other fields.

What We Do

Here is a look at our EDIT program

EDIT

Grade 9 students are first introduced to engineering and design concepts through the EDIT course. It teaches students how to think like an engineer and gives practical project based experience in programming, building and designing. After grade 9 edit students will take part in TMR3M, This grade 10 course takes the fundamentals and takes it to the next level. Students learn and compete in the First Tech Challenge where they will work alongside a student mentor to design, build and program a competition ready robot. In grade 11 students will once again compete in the First Tech Challenge but as a mentor. Grade 11 students will learn leadership and communication skills important for careers in the field. After grade 11 students will take IDC4U. IDC4U is the grade 12 class, this class hoans in on skills learned throughout the years you participated in the EDIT program to participate in the First Robotics Competition to engineer more complicated and involved mechanisms. The IDC4U class students will go on robotics trips to competitions and tour sponsor facilities.
